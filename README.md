# Skills

[简体中文](README.zh-CN.md)

Train the part of you AI can't replace.

When cars showed up, people kept going to the gym to work their legs — not because cars are slow, but because **thinking itself shouldn't be outsourced**. AI is an amplifier and a whetstone. It amplifies what you already have, and sharpens whatever you're willing to put in the time to build. The stronger you get, the more AI lifts you — **a spiral, going both ways**.

**This repo is about people, not tools.**

Most AI skills out there teach you to write better prompts, run faster scripts, ship slicker workflows. Useful — but those accumulate on their own, and they change too fast to stockpile.

The thing that's hard to accumulate is **judgment**. Knowing what's worth doing, what to drop, what users say they want but won't pay for, how to settle the direction debates that keep your team stuck. AI can't train that for you. It only grows in you through the loop: make a call, get the feedback, update the call.

I want these skills to sharpen the capabilities I think every engineer should build.

Most skills come from my own experience and ideas. Some are inspired by or adapted from work I came across elsewhere — when that's the case, the original is credited.
